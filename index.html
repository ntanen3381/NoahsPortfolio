All About Noah

Building an Android App

For Noah Tanenholtz, building an app using the Android SDK was a challenge.
He decided to take an online class and learn how to control the Android environment.
After he finished his class, he decided to take his aspirations of building a mobile app
to the next level. Noah built an app that would allow the user to convert different
measurements using the standard and metric system, and converting between each
system. Noah built his app and deployed it on the Google Play Store. Unfortunately,
Noah's app (Converjz) did not gain much steam on the app store and another problem
arised and would end his development of the app all together.

Trial and Error

At some point during his development, Noah occurred an error in Android Studio
(the IDE he was using to code his app) and decided to uninstall it thinking that his files
for his app would be saved locally during uninstalling and reinstalling of the IDE. Unfortunately,
he did not know of version control at the time and lost all of his files. All that Noah had
left of the app was its apk file. He tried to decompile the apk file but only found pre-compiled
code and knew that his app was corrupted and the development of his first mobile app was over
for good. From this learning experience, Noah grew his development skills and now uses Git for
everything and backs up all of his files to Github.

Startup Shell Shocked

This past semester Noah and two of his friends (Hassan and Peter) met in MATH 140
discussion class. Peter had built a homemade electric long board and it worked well,
but it had some design flaws. Peter has recently designed a new board that can fold
and after one prototype built, Lunation Boards LLC was born. All three of us applied
and went through an interview with Start Up Shell. They are now our incubator.

Lunation Boards LLC

Noah is currently the Co-Founder, COO, and Lead Programmer at Lunation Boards
